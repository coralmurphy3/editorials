Puerto Rican literature is flourishing as writers find new spaces to publish their work, as well as sell it across the Caribbean archipelago.

Puerto Rico has seen a proliferation of bookstores and activities to highlight authors and reach communities outside the metropolitan area – El Candil, a bookstore in Ponce, has started organizing activities for authors with recent publications, and rural towns like Aguadilla, Camuy, and Barranquitas are also celebrating bookstore openings.

Along with new shops, a handful of new, independent editorials are arriving at the scene, while older publishing houses continue to stand their ground as they benefit from online platforms to sell their books.

But is this burgeoning space in Puerto Rican literature opening in the same way for women compared to male authors when it comes to publishing?

The short answer: It depends.

Gaviota, Huracán, Tiempo Nuevo, El Instituto de Cultura Puertorriqueña and Callejón are the biggest publishing houses in Puerto Rico. Overall, they've published nearly 600 books foucused on Puerto Rican literature, poetry, history, and a variety of other topics.

The biggest gender gap falls under Gaviota, which is also the editorial with the most publications.
